Melissa Ho

Melissa Ho is a Specialist in Agricultural Policy at the Congressional Research Service in Washington D.C.

She covers issues related to international agricultural development, global food security, agricultural trade, climate adaptation, and agricultural research, education and extension for the U.S. Congress. Before coming to CRS, Melissa was a Program Officer in Agricultural Development at the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation. Previously, she served as the Agricultural Policy Advisor for Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton. Melissa has a Ph.D. in Plant Physiology from Penn State University, an M.Sc. in Soil Science from the University of California, Davis, and a B.Sc. in Environmental Systems from Cornell University.

Srikant Vasan

Srikant Vasan is the President of MyCollege Foundation,

A new non-profit that aims to help low income youth across America gain quality college credentials more affordably. Earlier, he was an Entrepreneur-in-Residence at the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, where he incubated his startup and advised the foundation's post-secondary team on strategy and investments. Earlier, he was a Senior Program Officer in the Global Development Program of the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, where he made investments related to using information and communications technologies to assist agricultural development as well as agricultural input value chains in sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia. Before joining the foundation, Srikant had a successful track record as an entrepreneur and in the corporate world, across multiple industries (software, Internet, cable, wireless telecom, and management consulting).

As an entrepreneur, Srikant was co-founder & CEO of StorePerform Technologies, the leading provider of retail performance management software, which he helped grow and then sell to a top supply chain software company. With offices in the US, UK, Mexico and India, StorePerform made a tangible impact on improving the operations of several of the world's top retailers in North America and Europe. Previously, Srikant was an Entrepreneur-in- Residence with Battery Ventures and Mobius Venture Capital, two of the leading venture capital firms in the US. Earlier, as Founder & CEO of KBkids (now called eToys after it purchased the assets of eToys), Srikant helped grow the company from a start-up founded in his basement to a run-rate of $100 million in revenue, successfully transitioning from a pure-play Internet retailer to one of the first successful clicks-and-mortar retailers, and later sold the company to a top toy retailer. Srikant was a finalist in Ernst & Young's 2005 Entrepreneur of the Year - Rocky Mountain Region, was included in Internet World's list of Top 25 Clicks and Mortar Executives in 2000, and was recognized as one of five emerging stars showcased in Software 2006, the leading industry conference on enterprise software.

Earlier in his career, Srikant was a member of the founding team and director of business development of MediaOne, a large cable company, where he evaluated investment/acquisition opportunities in the cable industry. As Director of Strategy at US West, he co-wrote US West Cellular's initial business plan for wireless data, and led the development of some e-commerce services at US West Interactive Services. He consulted to several Fortune 500 companies as a consultant at McKinsey & Co., and was an executive trainee at Tata Industries in India.

Srikant has an MBA from the Wharton School and a BS (Electrical Engineering) from the Indian Institute of Technology, Delhi. He has traveled extensively in six continents and loves the outdoors, reading, and theater. He is a Charter Member of TiE (The Indus Entrepreneurs), a global not-for-profit network of entrepreneurs and professionals dedicated to the advancement of entrepreneurship, and a co-founder of its TiE-Rockies chapter. He lives with his wife and three children in Denver.
