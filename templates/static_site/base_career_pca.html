Program Coordinator - Africa
Digital Green is an international, non-profit development organization that builds and deploys information and communication technology (ICT) to amplify the effectiveness of development efforts to affect sustained, social change. The Digital Green system combines technology and social organization to improve the cost-effectiveness and broaden the community participation in existing best practices. Digital Green is supported by Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation, Department for International Development (DFID), Ford Foundation, and Government of India. Digital Green's India country office is located in New Delhi with state offices in Bhopal, Bhubaneswar, Patna, Hyderabad and Bengaluru. It currently works in collaboration with local partners in seven states in India (Andhra Pradesh, Bihar, Jharkhand, Karnataka, Orissa, Madhya Pradesh, and Uttar Pradesh), and is currently extending its operations in Ethiopia, Ghana, and other parts of Sub-Saharan Africa.

To coordinate and provide leadership to its efforts in Africa, Digital Green is inviting applications for the position of Program Coordinator - Africa based in Addis Ababa. The Program Coordinator – Africa will oversee the design and implementation of a set of agriculture, health, and livelihood-related projects in partnership with government departments and civil society organizations across the continent.

This position is open to Ethiopian nationals or those who have a right to work in Ethiopia.

Position Summary:
  • Provide strategic direction, management, and coordination of the development, successful implementation, and monitoring of Digital Green's agriculture, livelihood and health programs across Africa (starting with a focus in Ethiopia)
  • Drive the establishment of partnerships between relevant institutions to define project objectives, work plans, budgets, and institutional arrangements.
  • Liaison with Digital Green’s senior management, quality assurance, and monitoring and evaluation directorates in India to share learnings and customize as appropriate
  • Supervise, monitor and coordinate the work of Digital Green’s staff, consultants, and technical advisory committee across the continent
  • Lead and support new project design and development across Africa
Key Responsibilities:
  • Identify, evaluate and seize opportunities to engage with government departments, donors, and civil society organizations and other stakeholders to develop projects and drive them
  • Drive partnerships between relevant institutions to define project objectives, work plans, budgets, and institutional arrangements.
  • Develop and manage a portfolio of complex partnerships requiring high-level management skills, and monitoring and evaluation.
  • Manage partnerships with government, research, and other stakeholders at a national- and international-levels
  • Communicate strategies internally and externally to manage progress, respond to midcourse corrections, and deliver on key milestones, outputs, and outcomes on a timely basis
  • Integrate inputs of programmatic and training leads to effectively and efficiency manage project activities
Experience and Skills:
  • Master's degree in rural management, development, and/or communications
  • 10-12 years of experience in program development, implementation, and management of agricultural, health, and rural development activities in Ethiopia
  • Established rapport with Ministries of Agriculture and Health and relevant stakeholders in Ethiopia and other African countries
  • Understanding of government structures, agricultural extension policies, protocols and guidelines
  • Rigorous capacity to think through, evaluate, justify, and make major decisions to manage projects
  • Established skills in guiding and providing technical assistance to government and civil society partnerships working on development programs, particularly around information and communication technologies and behavior change communication.
  • Able representative of organization in high-level meetings with donors, partners, and other stakeholders
  • Experience developing, managing, and implementing project strategies and the negotiation, execution, conduct, and managing of performance-based contracts
  • Adept to work with global, regional, and country partners
  • Record of working well with others in teams; sense of humor and humility
  • Ability to travel frequently across Africa
All positions carry competitive salaries with fringe benefits comparable to leading national level development sector organizations. Desirous candidates should apply with a cover note that highlights their motivation to apply and suitability for the position applied, current CVs and three references to jobs@digitalgreen.org
