Eric Walker
As vice president of corporate services, Eric Walker is responsible for the implementation of the organization's financial and administrative operations. He provides leadership for the finance, human resources, information systems, facilities, agreements, and risk management functions of PATH's headquarters, branch, and field offices. Mr. Walker has over 30 years experience in financial and administrative management, institutional capability building, and systems development. He has comprehensive expertise in financial training topics and methodologies and has provided training to international field offices, and local non-government organizations (NGOs) in developing countries in audit, United States Agency for International Development (USAID) compliance, accounting, software, financial management, and other topics. Mr. Walker has worked extensively in developing systems to facilitate the financial and overall management of USAID-funded contracts, grants, and cooperative agreements. He has broad-based knowledge of the applicable rules and regulations of each type of funding arrangement. He has created several 501(c)(3) tax-exempt organizations based in the United States and assisted in establishing NGOs in developing countries. Previously, Mr. Walker was director of finance and administration for the Overseas Education Fund, Inc., and controller at International Voluntary Services in Washington, DC. His experience also includes a position at the Development Group for Alternative Policies, including training activities in Asia and Latin America and accounting software development and support. He is a founder and past chairman of InsideNGO, a United States organization working in international development. Mr. Walker has an MA in Latin American studies from Georgetown University.
