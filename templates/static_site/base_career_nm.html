Nutrition Program Manager
The Strengthening Partnerships, Results and Innovations in Nutrition Globally (SPRING) Project is a United States Agency for International Development (USAID)-funded project which began October 1, 2011. It is implemented by five experienced and respected nutrition and public health organizations, including: JSI Research & Training Institute, Inc. (JSI), the managing partner; Helen Keller International (HKI); the International Food Policy Research Institute (IFPRI); Save the Children (SC); and the Manoff Group (TMG). The main focus of SPRING is the reduction of anemia and stunting among women and young children through the expansion of effective social and behavior change communication (SBCC) and nutrition-sensitive programs aimed at promoting high impact maternal, infant and young child nutrition (MIYCN) practices. Under this mandate, SPRING has committed to identifying, adapting and testing highly promising SBCC tools and models in several priority countries. The first model to be tested is an innovative participatory video learning approach currently being used by Digital Green to promote the diffusion of improved agricultural practices in India. SPRING proposes to adapt and test this approach for nutrition in the Indian state of Orissa. High impact nutrition behaviors that will be promoted will be determined based on local priorities, but are likely to include exclusive breastfeeding, timely and appropriate complementary feeding, diversification of diets, micronutrient supplementation, household gardening, safe water and hygiene, among other topics.

SPRING's partner for this project, Digital Green, is an international, non-profit development organization that builds and deploys information and communication technology (ICT) to amplify the effectiveness of development efforts to affect sustained, social change. The Digital Green system combines technology and social organization to improve the cost-effectiveness and broaden the community participation in existing best practices. Digital Green is supported by Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation, Department for International Development (DfiD), Ford Foundation, and Government of India. Digital Green's India country office is located in New Delhi with state offices in Bhopal, Bhubaneswar, Patna, Hyderabad and Bengaluru. It currently works in collaboration with local partners in seven states in India (Bihar, Jharkhand, Orissa, Madhya Pradesh, and Karnataka), and is currently extending its operations to Ghana and Ethiopia, in Africa.

Digital Green , in partnership with SPRING, is inviting applications from suitable candidates for the position of Nutrition Program Manager, to be based in Bhubaneswar, the capital of Orissa State. The successful candidate will have a university degree in human nutrition and/or public health, with a focus on maternal, infant and young child nutrition, and/or health education/communication, plus a minimum of four to five years of relevant experience in project/program management and/or implementation at state or district levels. Good reading and writing skills in both English and Oriya are essential. Research experience is considered valuable. The successful candidate will be initially contracted for a period of 12 months but this may as well be extended.

Key Responsibilities:
  • Support the nutrition training and ongoing orientation of a district-based partner organization and village-level teams conducting in an innovative behavior change communication project
  • Participate in the review of a research protocol and instruments related to the proposed formative research, and ensure the accuracy and consistency of nutrition-related language
  • Participate in the orientation and field supervision of a local research team
  • Provide quality assurance during the collection and analysis of qualitative data by the research team, and participate in the review and interpretation of the results
  • Provide oversight of the annual work plan for the project to ensure timely execution of all planned activities, including monthly field visit to the project site to discuss
  • Using the SPRING/Digital Green project management system, drive project activities and report on activities, milestones and accomplishments in a timely fashion
  • Nurture and strengthen partnership with the local NGO partner, the community groups, and other stakeholders involved with the project
  • Identify concerns, issues and problems promptly, address and report them to both SPRING and the Digital Green Orissa State Manager
  • Liaise with partners and technical advisors in the selection and prioritization of key nutrition focused and nutrition-sensitive practices, to be used in the development of content for locally produced videos
  • Provide feedback to local video production team on storyboards and draft videos produced on key nutrition practices, ensuring the accuracy of the content and consistency of the information presented with the national and state-level nutrition agenda.
  • Act as a technical resource to the local NGO partner and their community teams, answering questions and providing information needed during the project.
Qualification and Skills:
  • A university degree in human nutrition or public health with a maternal, infant and young child nutrition focus; and/or health education/communication, and/or research
  • Four to five years of relevant experience in project/program management and/or implementation at state or district levels
  • Good reading and writing skills in both English and Oriya
  • Excellent communication and negotiation skills; ability to prepare clear and concise reports
  • Strong strategic and creative thinking
  • Excellent leadership, coordination and information management skills
  • Demonstrated analytical skills applied on a wide range of factors, parameters, policies and priorities for the assessment and resolution of complex situations
  • Personal commitment, drive for results, efficiency and flexibility
